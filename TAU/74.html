74. The Delusion of Coercing.

When the people do not fear death, to what purpose is death still used as a punishment to overawe them?

And should the people be kept in continual fear of death, and I lay hold of those who are not to be so intimidated and slay them, who would dare?

There is always the Great Executioner.

Now for any man to act the Executioner's part, I say, it is hewing out the Great Architect's work for him.

And he who undertakes to hew for the Great Architect rarely fails to cut his hands.
