03. Giving the People Rest

Not exalting worth avoids rivalry.

Not prizing value avoids theft.

Not looking on objects of lust keeps the heart from chaos.

The government of the sage consists, accordingly:
  • in emptying the desires and filling the stomach
  • in weakening the will and strengthening the bones
  • in continually resolving hate
  • and in making those who hold hate not dare to act.


He acts with inaction, and so nothing is ungoverned.
